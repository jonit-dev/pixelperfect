## Understanding Why Photos Become Blurry

Before fixing blur, you need to identify its cause—different blur types require different solutions.

![Person taking a photo in motion](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1502982720700-bfff97f2ecac?w=800&q=80)
*Understanding the source of blur is the first step to fixing it.*

### The 5 Types of Image Blur

| Blur Type | Cause | Appearance | Fixability |
|-----------|-------|------------|------------|
| Motion blur | Camera shake | Directional streaking | Moderate |
| Focus blur | Wrong focus point | Soft, hazy areas | Moderate |
| Resolution blur | Low pixel count | Pixelated when enlarged | High |
| Compression blur | Heavy JPEG artifacts | Blocky, smeared details | Moderate |
| Depth of field | Intentional bokeh | Smooth background blur | Limited |

### Quick Diagnosis

**Motion Blur**: Look for directional streaks. Moving subjects appear stretched in one direction.

**Focus Blur**: The subject is uniformly soft while something else (foreground/background) is sharp.

**Resolution Blur**: Image becomes pixelated when zoomed to 100%. Common with old photos or screenshots.

**Compression Blur**: Visible blocks or smearing, especially around edges. Check if source was heavily compressed JPEG.

**Depth of Field**: Background is smoothly blurred while subject is sharp (or vice versa if focus missed).

<Callout type="info">
Resolution blur is the most fixable type—AI can intelligently add detail. Motion and focus blur are more challenging because information is truly lost, not just lacking.
</Callout>

## Method 3: AI Deblurring for Motion Blur

Motion blur occurs when the camera or subject moves during exposure. AI can sometimes reverse this, but results vary.

### How Motion Deblurring Works

AI motion deblurring attempts to:
1. Detect the direction and magnitude of blur
2. Calculate a "deconvolution kernel"
3. Mathematically reverse the blur effect
4. Fill in missing detail with learned patterns

### Realistic Expectations

| Motion Severity | Success Rate | Quality |
|-----------------|--------------|---------|
| Slight shake | 70-80% | Good |
| Moderate motion | 40-60% | Fair |
| Severe blur | 10-30% | Limited |
| Subject + camera motion | <20% | Poor |

### Best Practices

- **Provide context**: Use tools that understand the image content
- **Try multiple tools**: Different AI models have different strengths
- **Accept limitations**: Severely blurred images may not be recoverable
- **Combine methods**: Deblur first, then upscale for best results

<Callout type="warning">
Motion deblurring AI has improved dramatically but still has limits. If the blur is severe, the original detail simply doesn't exist in the image data to recover.
</Callout>

## Method 4: Focus Enhancement with AI

Out-of-focus blur is mathematically similar to motion blur but uniform in all directions.

### Why Focus Blur Is Challenging

When a camera misses focus, light rays don't converge properly on the sensor. The resulting "circles of confusion" spread information across multiple pixels, losing detail permanently.

### What AI Can Do

| Scenario | AI Capability |
|----------|---------------|
| Slightly soft focus | Can significantly improve |
| Moderate blur | Can partially improve |
| Completely out of focus | Limited recovery |
| Bokeh background | Should not "fix" (intentional) |

### Enhancement Approach

For slight focus issues:
1. Start with AI upscaling (adds detail)
2. Apply mild sharpening
3. Adjust contrast to enhance perceived sharpness

For moderate focus blur:
1. Use specialized deblurring AI
2. Accept that some softness may remain
3. Consider if the image is usable at smaller display sizes

## Method 5: JPEG Artifact Removal

Heavy JPEG compression creates distinctive "blur" patterns—blocks, smearing, and color banding.

### Identifying Compression Artifacts

- Visible 8×8 pixel blocks
- "Mosquito noise" around edges
- Color bleeding between areas
- Loss of fine texture detail

### AI Compression Artifact Removal

Modern AI can:

## What AI Cannot Fix

Let's be honest about limitations:

### Truly Unrecoverable Blur

- **Completely out of focus**: If there's no detail, AI can only guess
- **Extreme motion blur**: Information spread across dozens of pixels
- **Subject moved + camera moved**: Combined blur is very complex
- **Intentional blur**: Don't "fix" artistic bokeh or motion effects

### When to Accept Limitations

| Situation | Recommendation |
|-----------|----------------|
| Severe blur, important photo | Try AI, accept partial improvement |
| Severe blur, casual photo | May not be worth effort |
| Moderate blur, important | AI will likely help significantly |
| Slight blur, any photo | AI should fully resolve |

## Best Practices for Preventing Blurry Photos

While AI can fix many issues, prevention is always better:

### Camera Settings

- **Shutter speed**: Use 1/(focal length) minimum (e.g., 1/50s for 50mm lens)
- **ISO**: Don't fear higher ISO—noise is fixable, blur isn't
- **Aperture**: Smaller apertures (f/8-f/11) increase depth of field
- **Focus mode**: Use continuous AF for moving subjects

### Technique

- **Brace yourself**: Elbows in, lean against something solid
- **Breath control**: Shoot at the end of an exhale
- **Burst mode**: Take multiple shots, pick the sharpest
- **Tripod**: Use for any shutter speed below 1/60s

### Phone Photography

- **Tap to focus**: Ensure focus is on your subject
- **Hold steady**: Wait a moment after pressing shutter
- **Good lighting**: More light = faster shutter = less blur
- **Clean lens**: Smudges cause softness
