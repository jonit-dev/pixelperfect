## Common Real Estate Photo Problems

Real estate photography presents unique challenges:

### Problem 1: Dark/Underexposed Interiors

**Causes**:
- Mixed indoor/outdoor lighting (windows blow out)
- Insufficient natural light
- On-camera flash creating harsh shadows

**AI enhancement helps**:
- Brightens underexposed areas
- Recovers detail in bright windows
- Balances exposure across the image
- Reduces harsh shadows

### Problem 2: Lack of Sharpness and Detail

**Causes**:

- Wide-angle lens softness at edges
- Limited depth of field
- Camera shake

**AI enhancement helps**:

- Enhances texture in materials
- Improves overall clarity
- Makes features more appealing

### Problem 3: Small Image Size

**Causes**:

## MLS Photo Requirements and Standards

Before enhancing, understand your MLS requirements:

### Common MLS Specifications

| Platform | Minimum Size | Maximum Size | Format | Quality |
|----------|--------------|--------------|--------|----------|
| **Zillow** | 1024×768 | 15,000×15,000 | JPEG/JPG | High |
| **Realtor.com** | 1920×1080 | 20 MB file | JPEG/JPG | High |
| **MLS (varies)** | 640×480 | Varies | JPEG/JPG | Medium-High |
| **Redfin** | 1024×768 | 10 MB file | JPEG/JPG | High |

**Recommendation**: Aim for 1920×1280 (horizontal) or 1280×1920 (vertical) minimum. This meets all major platform requirements and leaves room for cropping.

<Callout type="info">**Size matters**: MLS platforms compress your photos. Starting with higher resolution means better quality after their compression.</Callout>

## Step-by-Step: Enhancing Real Estate Photos

### Step 1: Capture the Best Possible Original

AI enhancement works best when starting with quality originals:

#### Shooting Tips for Best Results

| Technique | How | Why |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| **Use natural light** | Open curtains, turn on lights | Brighter, more inviting |
| **Shoot during golden hour** | Morning/evening light | Warm, appealing glow |
| **Use a tripod** | Essential for sharp interiors | Eliminates camera shake |
| **Shoot at eye level** | 5-6 feet height | Most natural perspective |
| **Include a human element** | Agent or stager in shot | Shows scale, adds life |
| **Declutter first** | Remove personal items | Focus on property features |
| **Multiple angles** | 3-4 shots per room | Options to choose best |

#### Equipment Checklist

**Minimum**:
- Smartphone with good camera (iPhone 12+, recent Android)
- Natural light
- Steady hand or makeshift support

**Better**:
- DSLR or mirrorless camera
- Wide-angle lens (16-24mm equivalent)
- Basic tripod
- External flash (bounced)

**Professional**:
- Full-frame camera
- High-quality wide-angle lens
- Sturdy tripod
- Multiple speedlights or strobes
- HDR bracketing capability

### Step 2: Select Photos for Enhancement

Not every photo needs enhancement. Prioritize:

**Enhance these first**:
- Hero shot (exterior/front of house)
- Kitchen (most important room)
- Primary bathroom
- Living areas
- Master bedroom
- Outdoor living spaces
- Unique features (pool, view, etc.)

**Can skip enhancement**:
- Already excellent photos
- Utility rooms/closets
- Garage (unless notable)

## Common Real Estate Enhancement Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Over-Enhancement

**What happens**: Over-processed, unnatural appearance

**How to avoid**:
- Use 2x instead of 4x when quality is decent
- Review at 100% zoom
- Compare with original frequently
- When in doubt, less is more

### Mistake 2: Misrepresentation

**What happens**: Photos don't match reality, angry buyers

**How to avoid**:
- Enhance, don't transform
- Maintain accurate colors
- Don't remove permanent features
- Disclose any major edits if asked

### Mistake 3: Inconsistent Quality

**What happens**: Some photos great, others poor, unprofessional impression

**How to avoid**:
- Enhance all photos to similar level
- Use consistent processing
- Review entire set before publishing
- Replace weak photos rather than including them

### Mistake 4: Ignoring Composition

**What happens**: Great enhancement can't fix bad framing

**How to avoid**:
- Get composition right in camera
- Level horizons
- Straighten vertical lines
- Use rule of thirds
- Crop thoughtfully during editing

### Mistake 5: Wrong File Format or Size

**What happens**: Photos rejected by MLS or poor quality after upload

**How to avoid**:
- Export as highest quality JPEG
- Meet minimum size requirements
- Stay under maximum file sizes
- Test upload before batch submission

## Legal and Ethical Considerations

### What's Acceptable Enhancement

✅ **Generally acceptable**:
- Brightening/darkening
- Color correction (natural appearance)
- Sharpening
- Minor spot healing (temporary items)
- HDR blending (within reason)

❌ **Generally not acceptable**:
- Removing permanent features
- Adding features that don't exist
- Significant color alteration
- Misrepresenting condition
- Hiding defects

### Disclosure Best Practices

- **Maintain accuracy**: Photos should represent the property
- **Don't hide material facts**: Known issues should be visible or disclosed
- **Avoid misleading edits**: When in doubt, be conservative
- **Local regulations**: Some areas have specific rules about photo manipulation

<Callout type="warning">**When in doubt**: Consult your broker, association, or legal counsel. Misrepresentation can lead to liability and damage your reputation.</Callout>

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I use AI enhancement instead of professional photography?

For many listings, yes. AI enhancement bridges much of the gap between amateur and professional photography. However, for luxury properties or very high-end listings, professional photography may still be warranted.

### How many photos should a listing have?

**Typical ranges**:
- Starter home: 15-20 photos
- Average home: 20-30 photos
- Luxury home: 30-50+ photos

Quality beats quantity, but provide comprehensive coverage.

### Should I enhance every photo?

Prioritize based on importance. Hero shot, kitchen, primary bath, living areas, and master bedroom are essential. Secondary rooms can often use original photos if they're decent.

### Will enhanced photos look fake?

Quality AI enhancement like MyImageUpscaler produces natural results. Avoid over-processing by using appropriate enhancement levels and reviewing at 100% zoom.

### Can I enhance old listing photos to refresh the listing?

Yes! Re-processing existing photos through MyImageUpscaler can give a stale listing new life. Combine with a price reduction for maximum impact.

### How do I handle aerial/drone photos?

Enhance drone photos just like ground shots. Ensure you comply with all drone regulations and MLS rules for aerial photography.

### What file format should I use for MLS?

JPEG/JPG is standard for all major platforms. Use highest quality setting (95-100% in Lightroom, "maximum" in other tools) to minimize compression artifacts.
