Selling on Amazon or Etsy with low-resolution product photos is leaving money on the table. Both platforms favor high-quality images in search results, and buyers are far more likely to purchase when they can zoom in and see product details clearly.

The problem? Many sellers work with supplier images, phone photos, or older product shots that don't meet modern marketplace requirements. Here's how to fix that.

## Amazon and Etsy Image Requirements

Before upscaling, you need to know what you're aiming for.

![E-commerce product photography setup with camera and lightbox](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1523275335684-37898b6baf30?w=800&q=80)

### Amazon Requirements

| Requirement | Minimum | Recommended |
|-------------|---------|-------------|
| Resolution | 1000px (longest side) | 2000px+ |
| Format | JPEG, PNG, GIF, TIFF | JPEG (sRGB) |
| Background | Pure white (#FFFFFF) | Pure white |
| Product Coverage | 85% of frame | 85%+ |
| Zoom Enabled | 1000px minimum | 1600px+ for zoom |

Amazon's zoom feature only activates when images are at least 1000px on the longest side. But here's the catch: **1600px or higher** provides the best zoom experience, which directly impacts conversion rates.

### Etsy Requirements

| Requirement | Minimum | Recommended |
|-------------|---------|-------------|
| Resolution | 1000px (shortest side) | 2000px+ |
| Aspect Ratio | Any (5:4 displays best) | 5:4 or 4:3 |
| Format | JPEG, PNG, GIF | JPEG |
| File Size | Under 20MB | 1-5MB optimal |

Etsy thumbnails display at 570x456px, but full-size images should be much larger for the lightbox view.

## When to Upscale Product Photos

Upscaling makes sense in several common scenarios:

**1. Supplier-provided images**
Manufacturers often send product images at 500-800px. These need upscaling for both Amazon and Etsy.

**2. Older product catalogs**
If you shot products years ago at lower resolutions, upscaling saves a reshoot.

**3. Phone photos**
Quick smartphone shots might be 1200px—fine for Etsy but too small for optimal Amazon zoom.

**4. Cropped images**
Heavy cropping to remove backgrounds or reframe products reduces resolution significantly.

## Step-by-Step: Upscaling for Amazon

Here's the process for preparing Amazon-ready images:

![Online shopping and product browsing on laptop](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1556742049-0cfed4f6a45d?w=800&q=80)

### Step 1: Check Your Current Resolution

Before upscaling, verify your image dimensions:
- **Windows**: Right-click > Properties > Details
- **Mac**: Right-click > Get Info
- **Online**: Upload to any image editor

If your longest side is already 2000px+, you likely don't need to upscale.

### Step 2: Choose Your Target Resolution

For Amazon, aim for:
- **Minimum**: 1600px longest side (enables zoom)
- **Optimal**: 2000-2500px longest side

## Step-by-Step: Upscaling for Etsy

Etsy has slightly different considerations:

### Step 1: Consider Your Listing Style

Etsy buyers respond to different image styles:
- **Lifestyle shots**: Products in context (on a table, being worn, etc.)
- **Flat lays**: Arranged products from above
- **Detail shots**: Close-ups of craftsmanship

Each style may need different upscaling approaches.

### Step 2: Target the Right Dimensions

For Etsy's 5:4 display ratio:
- **Minimum**: 1000x800px
- **Optimal**: 2000x1600px or 2500x2000px

### Step 3: Maintain Aspect Ratio

When upscaling, preserve the original aspect ratio. Stretching images to fit Etsy's display creates distortion that buyers notice.

### Step 4: Optimize File Size

After upscaling:
1. Save as JPEG at 80-90% quality
2. Keep file size under 5MB for fast loading
3. Use sRGB color profile for consistent display

## Common Upscaling Mistakes to Avoid

### Over-upscaling

Upscaling a 200px image to 4000px won't create detail that wasn't there. AI can enhance and sharpen, but it can't invent accurate product details.

**Rule of thumb**: 4x upscaling is usually the practical maximum. Beyond that, results degrade.

### Ignoring Text and Labels

Generic upscalers often blur text on product packaging. If your product has important text (ingredients, specifications, brand names), verify it remains readable after upscaling.

### Inconsistent Image Sizes

Amazon and Etsy both benefit from consistent image dimensions across your listings. Upscale all images to the same target resolution for a professional appearance.

### Skipping Quality Check

Always zoom to 100% and inspect the upscaled image before uploading. Artifacts that seem invisible at thumbnail size become obvious in Amazon's zoom view.

## Beyond Upscaling: Complete Product Photo Workflow

Upscaling is one step in preparing marketplace-ready images:

1. **Capture/Source**: Get the best original quality possible
2. **Edit**: Remove backgrounds, adjust colors, crop
3. **Upscale**: Increase resolution to meet requirements
4. **Optimize**: Compress for web, apply correct color profile
5. **Upload**: Add to marketplace with proper alt text

Each step matters. A well-upscaled image with poor color balance still won't convert.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Will Amazon reject upscaled images?

No. Amazon evaluates image quality, not how you achieved it. If your upscaled image is sharp, correctly sized, and follows their guidelines, it's accepted.

### How much can I upscale before quality suffers?

Typically 2-4x is safe. Upscaling a 500px image to 2000px (4x) usually produces good results. Upscaling 500px to 4000px (8x) often shows noticeable degradation.

### Do I need different images for Amazon vs Etsy?

The main image requirements are similar. However, Etsy allows more lifestyle/contextual imagery, while Amazon requires pure white backgrounds for main images. You might use the same upscaled source with different backgrounds.

### My product photos have fine print—will upscaling blur it?

Standard upscaling often blurs fine text. Look for upscalers specifically designed to preserve text and fine details if your products include important labeling.
